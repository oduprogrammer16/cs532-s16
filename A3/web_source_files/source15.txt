<p class="western">Two things are responsible for human development: environmental influence and genetics, the latter having the greater influence. Although obvious things are influenced by genetics, such as athletic ability and intelligence, certain genetic traits are not as obvious.</p>
<h2 class="western">10. Laziness</h2>
<p class="western"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-145" src="http://www.viralcycle.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/lazy.jpg" alt="lazy" width="500" height="382" srcset="http://www.viralcycle.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/lazy-300x229.jpg 300w, http://www.viralcycle.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/lazy.jpg 500w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px" /></p>
<p class="western">Some people are abnormally lazy, preferring to laze on the couch and watch movies rather than working. They just cannot seem to shake this habit. It is not necessarily their fault, however, as genetics can play a role.</p>
<p class="western">Researchers who bred two groups of rats found a laziness gene by interbreeding active and less active rats respectively. Over a few generations, it was found that those with active predecessors could run for longer periods of time, while those whose ancestors were less active, ran for a lessor period. They found that there were many factors ascribed to genetics, such as mitochondria levels, physical characteristics, etc. The greatest reason for their activity levels, however, was found in a set of 36 genes.</p>
<p class="western">Laziness, therefore, may have an excuse.</p>
