drove his pickup truck up to a berm, stepped out of the vehicle and dropped the tailgate. Seconds later, a neighbor told authorities, the earth opened and swallowed him.</p> <p>The neighbor, who was in her garden, “saw his arms go up in the air,” Maricopa County Sheriff’s Det. Doug Matteson told The Washington Post. “She didn’t see him get up, so she called 911.”</p> <p>Police said 60-year-old Guadalupe Gomez Nila was killed Friday afternoon when he was swallowed by the sinkhole — which measured 15 feet wide and 12 to 18 feet deep.</p> <p>“It was a freak act of nature,” Matteson said. “It’s just a sad scene. When the hole opened up, it was almost like being struck by lightning. … Our heart goes out to his family.”</p> <p channel="wp.com" class="interstitial-link"> <i> [<a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/post-nation/wp/2016/01/29/its-a-monster-for-sure-sinkhole-closes-part-of-oregon-highway/">A ‘monster’ 80-foot sinkhole closed a stretch of coastal highway in Oregon</a>] </i> </p> <div class="flex-embed"> <blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="500"> <p dir="ltr" lang="en">Coming up at 10: Hear from the family of Guadalupe Gomez Nilas who was killed after falling in a sinkhole <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/12News?src=hash">#12News</a> <a href="https://t.co/LhMmxgFQJH">pic.twitter.com/LhMmxgFQJH</a></p> <p>— Charly Edsitty (@CharlyEdsitty) <a href="https://twitter.com/CharlyEdsitty/status/696176488907800576">February 7, 2016</a></p></blockquote> <p><script async="" charset="utf-8" src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js"></script></p></div> <p>When authorities arrived Friday afternoon at the private farm in Queen Creek, Ariz., not far from Phoenix, Nila’s truck was still running and his tools were on the ground. The sinkhole, they said, was filling with rushing water.</p> <p>Officials said Nila was working on private property owned by Sossaman Farms. Matteson, the sheriff’s detective, said authorities believe Nila was planning to shut off the water or check on it when the ground split open.</p> <p>Rescue efforts continued throughout the evening. By about 6 p.m., authorities said, they had located a body.</p> <p>Nila most likely suffocated or drowned, though the coroner has not yet determined the official cause of death.</p> <p>Constance Halonen-Wilson, public information officer for the town of Queen Creek, said officials believe the sinkhole was an isolated incident.</p> <p>“Crews worked to repair and fill the hole on Saturday,” she told The Post in a statement. “We are working with the Arizona State Geological Survey Office to ensure the area is safe.”</p> <p channel="wp.com" class="interstitial-link"> <i> [<a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2015/08/19/florida-sinkhole-reopens-two-years-after-it-swallowed-a-sleeping-man-and-killed-him/">Florida sinkhole reopens two years after it swallowed a sleeping man and killed him</a>] </i> </p> <p>Nila’s daughters told local news media he was the sole provider for his family.</p> <p>“It was devastating — it still is devastating,” Sonia Hernandez, one of Nila’s eight children, <a href="http://www.12news.com/story/news/local/arizona/2016/02/07/daughters-sinkhole-victim-tearfully-remember-father/79960596/">told NBC affiliate 12News</a>. “We’re still in shock.”</p> <p>Another daughter, Josephine Ruiz, told the station that the family is mourning.</p> <p>“It’s going to be hard,” she said. “It’s still very hard, unbelievable.
