<p>The Championship season has begun for Division 2 swimming with the Northeast 10 Championships.</p>
<h2>1. 2016 Northeast 10 Conference Championships</h2>
<p>The Southern Connecticut Sate University men&#8217;s team took first place and the Assumption College took the women&#8217;s Championship title.</p>
<div class="oembed-wrap">
<blockquote data-secret="veA5rpKD4j" class="wp-embedded-content"><a href="https://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/news/183585-2/">Assumption Women and Southern Connecticut Men Win the Northeast 10 Championships</a></p></blockquote>

<h2>2. NYU Invite</h2>
<p>LIU Post&#8217;s women&#8217;s team competed this Saturday at the NYU Invite against a multitude of other D3 teams. The team had a number of top three finishes including senior <strong>Joyce Kwok</strong>&#8216;s 100 butterfly. She went a 56.97 and made the NCAA B cut with this time. Another Pioneer, <strong>Seren Jones</strong>, came in third in the event (1:01.01).</p>
<p>The Pioneers also took first in the 100 back and breaststroke. <strong>Meghan Brazier</strong> won the backstroke event in a time of 59.50. Teammate Jones finished right behind her in a time of 59.70 for second place.</p>
<p>Freshman <strong>Caitlin Johnstone</strong> won the breaststroke in the time of 1:07.57. Another Pioneer freshman, <strong>Shannon de la Garza</strong> came in third in the event with a time of 1:16.80.</p>
<p><strong>Eline Swensen</strong> came in third in the 200 freestyle with time of 2:00.26.</p>
<p>The LIU relay teams won both the 200-yard medley relay and 800 freestyle relay. The 200 medley team of Jones (27.55), Johnstone (30.43), Kwok (25.90) and Brazier (24.36) won with the time of 1:48.24. Then, in the 800 freestyle relay Johnstone (1:59.50), Swensen (2:00.20), Fuller (1:59.89) and <strong>Kat Kazaba</strong> (2:05.85) won with a 8:05.44.</p>
