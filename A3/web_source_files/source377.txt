Fitness headphones, turntables, and something called a Boomstick are just some of the things we&#8217;ll cover in this month&#8217;s Sound Guys Monthly.</p>

If you&#8217;re not Shehan don&#8217;t worry, we&#8217;re doing another giveaway this month. Keep on reading to find out how to enter.</p><div class="post_content_ad left aa_tablet"></div>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/new-jaybird-freedom-x3-ears-remember-eq-preferences-7891/" target="_blank">Jaybird announces the new Jaybird X3&#8217;s and Jaybird Freedoms</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/new-jaybird-freedom-x3-ears-remember-eq-preferences-7891/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-7907 size-full" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Jaybird-Freedom.jpg" alt="Jaybird Freedom" width="792" height="400" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Jaybird-Freedom-300x152.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Jaybird-Freedom.jpg 792w" sizes="(max-width: 792px) 100vw, 792px" /></a></p>
<p>At the beginning of January Jaybird announced not one, but two new pairs of fitness headphones. Of course they announced the successors to the X2 headphones (aptly named the X3s) but they also announced a second pair of wireless buds called the Freedoms. The X3 have a 7 hour battery life while the Freedoms have a 4 hour battery. BUT, the Freedoms come with a small charging clip that adds another 4 hours of juice, totaling at 8 hours. Both have Bluetooth 4.0, are sweatproof, and have customizable EQ profiles that save to the hardware of the headphones. They’re not out yet but when they become available later this year, the X3 will cost $149 and the Freedoms $199.</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/misfit-introduces-new-specter-earbuds-7939/" target="_blank">Misfit is trying their hand at audio with their new Specter earbuds</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/misfit-introduces-new-specter-earbuds-7939/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-7944 size-full" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Misfit-Specter.jpg" alt="Misfit Specter" width="792" height="400" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Misfit-Specter-300x152.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Misfit-Specter-768x388.jpg 768w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Misfit-Specter.jpg 792w" sizes="(max-width: 792px) 100vw, 792px" /></a></p>
<p>Misfit took a slightly different approach when it came to these headphones as all of the tech that usually goes in the control module on the wire connecting the earbuds is found instead inside the clip. Of course, you’ll also be able to do fitness things like track your steps, distance walked, and calories burned. Misfit claims that the headphones have an 8-10 hour battery life and can also be used as a clicker to control your presentations, if you really want to be that guy. There’s no word on pricing yet but they will be available sometime this Spring.</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/jbl-armour-partner-two-new-headphones-8014/" target="_blank">JBL and Under Armour partner for the UA Headphones Wireless and UA Headphones Wireless Heart Rate</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/jbl-armour-partner-two-new-headphones-8014/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-8021 size-full" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/UA-Headphones-Wirelessnews.jpg" alt="UA Headphones Wireless[news]" width="792" height="400" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/UA-Headphones-Wirelessnews-300x152.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/UA-Headphones-Wirelessnews-768x388.jpg 768w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/UA-Headphones-Wirelessnews.jpg 792w" sizes="(max-width: 792px) 100vw, 792px" /></a></p>
<p>Everyone is trying to get in on the fitness market, so it’s no surprise to see JBL and Under Armour team up for two pretty cool pairs of wireless earbuds. The first are the UA Headphones Wireless which come in the classic in-ear form with an 8 hour battery life. The second are the UA Headphones Wireless Heart Rate which come in a clip-on flavor similar to the Powerbeats. These have a 5 hour battery life but can also track your heart rate and display the data accordingly in an app. Pretty cool stuff if you’re really into fitness. Both are sweatproof but not the same price. The UA Headphones Wireless are available now for $180 and the Heart Rate headphones are coming this Spring for $250.</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/boomstick-might-solution-bad-headphones-7818/" target="_blank">The Boomstick was announced at CES and could be the solution to bad headphones</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/boomstick-might-solution-bad-headphones-7818/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-7864 size-full" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Boomstick2.jpg" alt="Boomstick[2]" width="792" height="400" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Boomstick2-300x152.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Boomstick2.jpg 792w" sizes="(max-width: 792px) 100vw, 792px" /></a></p>
<p>Long story short, the Boomstick works similarly to a DAC. Plug it into your source device, then plug your headphones to the Boomstick and voila! Better audio. However the Boomstick differs from your typical DAC in that it doesn’t really convert the electrical signal into analog. Instead it uses algorithms to fine tune the signal to get maximum clarity based on the headphones that are plugged in. Think of it as a way to optimize your headphones rather than optimize the sound.</p>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/sony-announces-new-ps-hx500-turntable-ces-7870/" target="_blank">Sony&#8217;s announces the new PS-HX500 turntable. Long live the vinyl.</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/sony-announces-new-ps-hx500-turntable-ces-7870/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-7886 size-full" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Sony-PS-HX500.jpg" alt="Sony PS-HX500" width="792" height="400" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Sony-PS-HX500-300x152.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Sony-PS-HX500.jpg 792w" sizes="(max-width: 792px) 100vw, 792px" /></a></p>
<p>The new turntable is called the PS-HX500 and does more than just play your vinyl record, though that is a big part of it. Besides that, the turntable will also convert the vinyl into a hi-res audio file that you can play back in its full glory when you’re on-the-go, assuming of course that you can a device capable of storing and playing those files. Sony is quickly building an ecosystem around lossless playback and although I personally don’t have one of their hi-res walkmans, this turntable make me kind of want one.</p><div class="post_content_ad"></div>
<hr />
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/blue-lola-review-7862/" target="_blank">Best of January</a></h2>
<p><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/blue-lola-review-7862/"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-7875 size-large" src="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-1024x576.jpg" alt="Blue Lola[3]" width="1024" height="576" srcset="http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-300x169.jpg 300w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-1024x576.jpg 1024w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-16x9.jpg 16w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-32x18.jpg 32w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-64x36.jpg 64w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-712x400.jpg 712w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-1000x563.jpg 1000w, http://www.soundguys.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Blue-Lola3-1200x675.jpg 1200w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a></p>
<p>We were pretty busy this past month but we were still able to check out some pretty sick gear. The coolest thing we saw last month had to be the Lola headphones by Blue. Though we weren’t quite sure who the Lolas were for, that didn’t stop us from enjoying them. The headphones have a unique adjustment method and also just look pretty damn awesome. The black and white — actually the entire look in general is very Storm Trooper-esk and definitely fits the Star Wars vibe that has swept the world. They’re comfortable, have a really good sound, and do a great job at passively blocking outside noise. They’re not the most portable of things, but if you&#8217;re looking for a pair of over-ear cans to keep at your desk then they more than get the job done.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><a href="http://www.soundguys.com/anker-premium-bluetooth-speaker-review-7913/" target="_blank">Bang For Your Buck</a></h2>

<p>The Lolas aren’t extremely expensive, but they aren’t cheap. If you want something inexpensive but good, check out the Anker Premium, our pick for best bang for your buck. It should come as no surprise that Anker made yet another good product for not a lot of money. The Premium was one of the most asked about speakers we ever got so make sure to check out the full review if you want to know all of the gritty details. If you’re in a rush heres the cliff notes version: Looks nice, strong low end, kinda portable but would probably do better on your desk rather than your backpack. Oh and I still hate those damn buttons, but that can be overlooked thanks to sound quality.</p>
