                <h1>£2 MILLION a year lost on gambling machines in Liverpool’s Williamson Square</h1><a class="print-link" onclick="window.print()"><img src="http://www.stopthefobts.org/wp-content/themes/fobt-responsive/images/printer_icon.gif"/>Print this page</a>

            <p>Punters lost nearly £2m on gambling machines dubbed the ‘crack cocaine of gambling’ last year.</p>
<p>Gamblers racked up the heavy losses at 52 fixed-odds betting terminals (FOBTS) across just 13 bookies in or near Williamson Square in <a href="https://www.google.co.uk/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;uact=8&amp;ved=0ahUKEwiElbrw2-XKAhVldXIKHZW6A8QQFggfMAA&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.liverpoolecho.co.uk%2Fall-about%2Fliverpool-city-centre&amp;usg=AFQjCNEca_FKRN13Tqdw2zqQOyFXrQ1ASA&amp;bvm=bv.113370389%2Cd.bGQ">Liverpool city centre</a>.</p>
<p>Read the full story in the <a href="http://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/news/liverpool-news/2-million-year-lost-gambling-10853370">Liverpool Echo</a>.</p>
