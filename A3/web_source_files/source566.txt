				<h2 class="entry-title">Conversation in a jail cell with God</h2>
				<h3 class="entry-date">February 8, 2016</h3>
				<h3 class="entry-author"><a href="http://venturegalleries.com/author/calebpirtle/" title="Posts by Caleb Pirtle III" rel="author">Caleb Pirtle III</a></h3>
				<div class="full-content group">
					<figure id="attachment_73239" class="thumbnail wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 600px"><a href="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/Staccoto_Lightning.jpg"><img class="size-full wp-image-73239" src="http://venturegalleries.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/02/Staccoto_Lightning.jpg" alt="I expected thunder and lightning when I talked to God." width="600" height="717" /></a><figcaption class="caption wp-caption-text">I expected thunder and lightning when I talked to God.</figcaption></figure>
<p style="text-align: center;">Life is Fiction</p>
<p>HE LOOKED LIKE the one he pretended to be. Then again, he said he wasn’t pretending.</p>
<p>I could tell it when I looked into his eyes.</p>
<p>He believed.</p>
<p>No one else did.</p>
<p>No one else mattered.</p>
<p>He was dressed in a black suit with white shirt.</p>
<p>Not even a night in the Fort Worth jail had wrinkled his suit or soiled his shirt.</p>
<p>His black hair was slicked back.</p>
<p>It had touch of grease.</p>
<p>It had a touch of gray.</p>
<p>His shoes were spit shined.</p>
<p>Someone had removed his black tie.</p>
<p>He was angry about it.</p>
<p>He was mad enough to kill.</p>
<p>Might as well.</p>
<p>He had killed more than once.</p>
<p>At least that’s what the police said.</p>
<p>And he hadn’t denied it.</p>
<p>The newspaper’s deadline was three hours away when I sat down to talk with him.</p>
<p>I had taken a chance.</p>
<p>Some prisoners want to talk.</p>
<p>Most simply sit and stare at the wall.</p>
<p>Too many questions.</p>
<p>No answers at all.</p>
<p>They think I’ll tell the police if they tell me anything.</p>
<p>I won’t.</p>
<p>But police may read it in the evening edition.</p>
<p>I began with the same question I always do.</p>
<p>“What happened?” I ask.</p>
<p>He smiled.</p>
<p>His smile was cold.</p>
<p>His black eyes were piercing.</p>
<p>I felt them, and he knew it.</p>
<p>“When?” he asked.</p>
<p>“Last night.”</p>
<p>“What makes you think something happened last night?’ he asked.</p>
<p>“You’re in a cell now, and you weren’t then,” I said. “Something must have happened.”</p>
<p>He shrugged nonchalantly.</p>
<p>“I removed a man from this earth,” he said.</p>
<p>“How?”</p>
<p>“Does it matter?”</p>
<p>It didn’t.</p>
<p>I moved on.</p>
<p>“Why did you remove him?” I asked.</p>
<p>“It was his time.”</p>
<p>Why?</p>
<p>Always, why?</p>
<p>“He had sinned,” the prisoner said.</p>
<p>“Against God?”</p>
<p>“All sins are against God.”</p>
<p>“He could have changed his ways.”</p>
<p>“He had his chances.”</p>
<p>“Maybe he needed one more.”</p>
<p>The prisoner grinned.</p>
<p>“We all have our last one,” he said.</p>
<p>He shrugged again.</p>
<p>“He had wasted his last one,” he said.</p>
<p>The cell was dark.</p>
<p>It grew darker.</p>
<p>The cell was cold.</p>
<p>It grew colder.</p>
<p>“What did he do?” I asked.</p>
<p>“He chose the wrong Trinity,” the prisoner said.</p>
<p>I waited.</p>
<p>“Wine, women, and the lust for money,” he said.</p>
<p>“Your woman?”</p>
<p>The prisoner grinned again.</p>
<p>He shook his head.</p>
<p>“Your money?”</p>
<p>“No.”</p>
<p>His voice was soft.</p>
<p>The grin had not faded from his face.</p>
<p>“The wages of sin are death,” the prisoner said.</p>
<p>We sat in silence for a moment.</p>
<p>The silence grew louder.</p>
<p>I heard my blood running through my veins.</p>
<p>Maybe it was his blood, his veins.</p>
<p>“Vengeance is mine, saith the Lord,” he said.</p>
<p>“Is he the only one you’ve killed?</p>
<p>“What makes you think he&#8217;s the only one?”</p>
<p>“Did God tell you to kill him?”</p>
<p>“No.”</p>
<p>His voice was still soft.</p>
<p>“He didn’t have to,” he said.</p>
<p>“Why not?”</p>
<p>“I am God,” he said.</p>
<p>I waited for the thunder to roll.</p>
<p>It didn’t.</p>
<p>If the lightning flashed, it went somewhere else.</p>
<p>I didn’t laugh at him.</p>
<p>The jury did.</p>
<p>Twenty-five to life, the jury said.</p>
<p>He didn’t flinch.</p>
<p>Twenty-five to life, he told me, is nothing but a drop in the bucket for God.</p>
