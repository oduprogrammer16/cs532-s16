				<div class="post-inner">				<h1 class="name post-title entry-title" itemprop="itemReviewed" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Thing"><span itemprop="name">100 people still trapped in Taiwan quake rubble</span></h1>						
<p class="post-meta">
	
	<span class="post-cats">in  <a href="http://www.ripples.com.ng/category/news/international-news/" title="View all posts in International" rel="category tag">International</a>, <a href="http://www.ripples.com.ng/category/news/" title="View all posts in News" rel="category tag">News</a></span>
	
		
	 <span class="tie-date">February 8, 2016</span>	
<span class="post-views">219 Views</span> </p>
<div class="clear"></div>
					<div class="entry">										<div><b></b>Rescue efforts are still ongoing to pull out possible survivors with over a 100 people still thought to be trapped under the rubble of the collapsed building which crumbled after a 6.4 magnitude earthquake hit the southern Taiwan city of Tainan.</div>
<div><span style="color: #de0021;">Two people were rescued <span class="aBn" tabindex="0" data-term="goog_1916287482"><span class="aQJ">on Sunday</span></span> with time fast running out</span> for those still missing and are suspected to be buried deep under the rubble of the collapsed 17 storey building.</div>
<p><a href="http://www.ripples.com.ng/rescuers-find-signs-of-life-after-taiwan-earthquake/"><b> Read also: </b><span style="color: #e63f23;"> Rescuers find signs of life after Taiwan earthquake</span></a></p>
<div>
<p><a href="http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2016/02/rescued-taiwan-quake-rubble-time-runs-160208035025592.html" target="_blank"><span style="color: #0033cc3;"> A woman who was shielded under the body of her husband was rescued 48 hours after the quake struck.</span></a></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>&#8220;The rescued woman, Tsao Wei-ling, was found lying under her dead husband. Their two-year-old son, who was also killed, was found lying nearby.&#8221; Local legislator Wang Ting-yu told reporters <span class="aBn" tabindex="0" data-term="goog_1916287483"><span class="aQJ">on Monday</span></span>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A second survivor &#8211; Li Tsung-tian &#8211; had been pulled out of the rubble rescuers also confirmed <span class="aBn" tabindex="0" data-term="goog_1916287484"><span class="aQJ">on Monday</span></span>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Reporting from outside the collapsed building, Al Jazeera&#8217;s Rob McBride, revealed that rescue efforts had reached critical point.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>&#8220;It has been three days, and it&#8217;s understood that those stuck under the rubble and still alive won&#8217;t be able to last longer without food and water.</p>
</div>
<div>&#8220;Lots of questions are being asked about how this building was constructed. There are a lot of stories floating around about the builder. You could almost say he is the most wanted man in Taiwan right now. Everyone is trying to find him,&#8221; he said.</div>
<div></div>
