data-reactid=".ti.1.0.1.1.0.0.0.0.1.$p-0"><p>Hillary Clinton is campaigning as a <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/democratic-debate-transcript-clinton-sanders-omalley-in-south-carolina/">guardian</a> of President Barack <span id=":23n.1" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Obama&#8217;s</span> progressive policy accomplishments. In recent weeks, she has <a href="http://time.com/4183990/democratic-debate-bernie-sanders-hillary-clinton-obamacare/">called</a> the Affordable Care Act &#8220;one of the greatest accomplishments of President Obama, of the Democratic Party, and of our country,&#8221; and <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/18/us/politics/hillary-clinton-obama-debate.html">promised</a> that she is &#8220;going to defend Dodd-Frank&#8221; and &#8220;defend President Obama for taking on Wall Street.&#8221;</p>
<p>Meanwhile, however, Clinton’s campaign has been relying on a team of strategists and fundraisers, many of whom spent much of the last seven years as consultants or lobbyists for business interests working to obstruct Obama&#8217;s agenda in those two areas.<strong><br />
</strong></p>
<p>Consultants associated with the Dewey Square Group, a lobbying firm that has been retained by business interests to defeat a variety of progressive reforms, are playing a major role in the Clinton campaign. Charles Baker III, the co-founder of Dewey, is a senior strategist and the campaign&#8217;s chief administrative officer. Michael <span id=":23n.3" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Whouley</span>, another Dewey co-founder, played an <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/rubycramer/top-consulting-firm-emerges-in-early-hillary-clinton-efforts#.bn2xKJmAZ">early role</a> in advising Clinton&#8217;s plan for the current campaign by convening some of the very first strategy sessions. Senior Dewey officials Jill <span id=":23n.4" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Alper</span> and <span id=":23n.5" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Minyon</span> Moore are also close advisers and <a href="https://www.hillaryclinton.com/about/hillblazers/">fundraisers</a> for Clinton, while at least <a href="http://www.p2016.org/clinton/clintonorg.html#policy">four other</a> Clinton officials have worked at Dewey within the last four years. In addition, disclosures show that Clinton&#8217;s Super PACs Priorities USA Action and Correct the Record have also <a href="http://query.nictusa.com/cgi-bin/forms/C00578997/1046461/sb/29">paid</a> Dewey Square Group for a variety of services in this election.</p>
<p><strong>Undermining <span id=":23n.7" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Obamacare</span> reforms</strong></p>
<p>Dewey, for instance, worked on behalf of the health insurance industry during the health reform debate, specifically to block the changes to Medicare Advantage that were <a href="https://www.cms.gov/apps/docs/aca-update-implementing-medicare-costs-savings.pdf">critical</a> for financing the Affordable Care Act. Medicare Advantage, which allows Medicare beneficiaries to use plans administered by private insurers, had long served as a cash cow for the health insurance industry. By one estimate, insurance companies over-billed the government by nearly <a href="http://www.publicintegrity.org/2014/06/04/14840/why-medicare-advantage-costs-taxpayers-billions-more-it-should">$70 billion</a> in improper payments over just a five year period. Dewey, which had been tapped to by health insurers to block cuts from the program starting in 2007, continued during the Obama era to lobby to protect Medicare Advantage, even as such reforms became a major part of how Democrats and the Obama administration sought to finance the Affordable Care Act.</p>
<p>One of the more deceptive components of the Dewey lobbying strategy was uncovered when an editor at the Lawrence, Massachusetts, <em>Eagle-Tribune </em><a href="http://www.cjr.org/campaign_desk/a_laurel_to_the_eagletribune.php">realized</a> that the firm had worked quietly to place letters to the editor against cuts to Medicare Advantage under the names of elderly Massachusetts residents without their knowledge or consent.</p>
<p>Last year, <em><span id=":23n.8" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">ProPublica</span></em><span id=":23n.8" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">’s</span> Alec <span id=":23n.9" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">MacGillis</span> <a href="https://www.propublica.org/article/insurance-lobby-has-sturdy-bridges-to-democrats">pressed</a> America&#8217;s Health Insurance Plans, a major private health insurance lobby group that had retained Dewey, over the controversy. <span id=":23n.10" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">AHIP&#8217;s</span> spokesperson brushed off the fake letter incident as the work of an intern and told <span id=":23n.11" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">MacGillis</span> that <span id=":23n.12" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">AHIP</span> ended its relationship with Dewey back in 2008, before the letters were sent. But tax documents reviewed by <em>The Intercept</em> show that a state-level health insurance <a href="http://www.mahp.com/about-us/mahp-board-members.html">lobby group</a> called the Massachusetts Association of Health Plans, which represents the same major health insurance firms as <span id=":23n.13" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">AHIP</span>, <a href="http://pdfs.citizenaudit.org/2010_09_EO/22-2518016_990O_200912.pdf">continued</a> to pay Dewey throughout 2009 to 2012 for &#8220;grassroots&#8221; consulting.</p>
<p>&#8220;I don’t have any comment for your piece,&#8221; wrote Ginny Terzano, a spokesperson for Dewey, in response to an inquiry from <em>The Intercept</em>.</p>
<p>In 2009, Dewey was also retained by the National Restaurant Association, which at the time was lobbying aggressively to block health reform rules to require employers to provide health coverage to their employees. The National Restaurant Association, derided by labor activists as &#8220;the other NRA&#8221; for its role in lobbying against efforts to raise the minimum wage, paid Dewey <a href="http://pdfs.citizenaudit.org/2010_12_EO/36-1525480_990O_200912.pdf">$772,110</a> that year. Charles Baker, the Dewey co-founder who now serves as a senior strategist to the Clinton campaign, was also <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=ddb30cdf-ca82-4a82-96f9-6409b62bb7c1&amp;filingTypeID=51">registered</a> to influence health reform on behalf of the Medicines Company, a drug firm.</p>
<p>Other lobbyists now closely associated with the Clinton campaign were active in the fight against <span id=":23n.14" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Obama&#8217;s</span> health reforms.</p>
<p>Heather <span id=":23n.15" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Podesta</span> is one of the most prolific fundraisers for the Clinton campaign, having personally raised at least <a href="http://docquery.fec.gov/cgi-bin/forms/C00575795/1047311/sa/3L?platform=hootsuite">$348,581</a>, according to recently filed disclosures. In 2009 and 2010, <span id=":23n.16" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Podesta</span> worked as lobbyist for the health insurance company <span id=":23n.17" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Cigna</span>. While working for <span id=":23n.19" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Cigna to</span> <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=A13D0957-6EA9-4038-8A3F-A62C51536DB8&amp;filingTypeID=69">influence</a> the health reform bill, <span id=":23n.20" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Cigna</span> was one of several large health insurers to secretly provide over <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2010-11-17/insurers-gave-u-s-chamber-86-million-used-to-oppose-obama-s-health-law">$86 million</a> in secret payments used to air negative television and radio advertisements to defeat the law. Heather Podesta is one of several lobbyists now fundraising for the campaign who previously worked for insurance interests to influence health reform. Irene <span id=":23n.22" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Bueno</span>, a fundraiser who raised $26,675, <a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/lobby/firm_reports.php?id=D000036296&amp;year=2010">lobbied</a> on behalf of <span id=":23n.23" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">CareMore</span> (a division of health insurer Anthem) and Blue Shield of California to influence health reform as the bill was being debated. Bueno and Podesta did not respond to a request for comment.</p>
<p><strong>Helping big banks undermine financial reform</strong></p>
<p>On financial reform, Clinton has similarly tied herself to <span id=":23n.26" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Obama&#8217;s</span> legacy. Speaking with MSNBC&#8217;s Rachel Maddow last month, Clinton asserted that on Dodd-Frank, Obama&#8217;s financial reform legislation, she is one of the &#8220;many Democrats&#8221; who are &#8220;fighting to prevent it from being turned back.&#8221;</p>
<p>Clinton&#8217;s inner circle, however, has lobbied to help obstruct and roll back many of Dodd-Frank&#8217;s signature reforms.</p>
<p>The Benenson Strategy Group, the consulting firm run by Joel <span id=":23n.30" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Benenson</span>, now serving as the Clinton campaign&#8217;s chief pollster and strategist, was retained by the Financial Services Forum, a lobbying group for Wall Street interests such as <span id=":23n.31" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Citigroup</span> and Goldman Sachs. Lobbying records show the Financial Services Forum has worked over the years to weaken a variety of Dodd-Frank reforms. In 2013, the Financial Services Forum paid <span id=":23n.33" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Benenson&#8217;s</span> firm <a href="http://pdfs.citizenaudit.org/2014_12_EO/52-2241644_990O_201312.pdf">$273,459</a> while it <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=EE34088F-4F16-448E-91A7-1538B07C96B7&amp;filingTypeID=51">was lobbying</a> on a number of rules that were mandated by <span id=":23n.34" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Dodd</span>-Frank, including capital requirements designed to prevent another financial crisis. <span id=":23n.35" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Danny Franklin, a partner with the Benenson Stategy Group, wrote to <em>The Intercept</em> to say the Financial Services Forum is not currently a client of his firm, but declined to comment any further. </span></p>
<p>Last month, <span id=":23n.36" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Benenson</span> <a href="http://www.ibtimes.com/political-capital/hillary-clinton-strategist-attacks-bernie-sanders-anti-wall-street-ad-while-his">convened</a> a conference call with reporters to &#8220;deride Bernie Sanders for airing an ad that criticized Wall Street firms and the politicians who accept their donations,&#8221; according to a report from <em>International Business Times</em>. As <em><span id=":23n.37" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">IBTimes</span></em> reported, <span id=":23n.38" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Benenson</span> has also represented JPMorgan Chase and Bank of America, among other corporate clients.</p>
<p>Steve <span id=":23n.39" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Elmendorf</span>, a campaign <a href="http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gail-sullivan/steve-elmendorf-monsanto_b_7724814.html">adviser</a> and fundraiser who has collected <a href="http://docquery.fec.gov/cgi-bin/forms/C00575795/1047311/sa/3L">$30,505</a> for Clinton, was retained by Goldman Sachs as one of the bank&#8217;s &#8220;<a href="http://www.investmentnews.com/article/20100427/FREE/100429903/goldman-rolls-out-the-big-guns-in-lobbying-blitz">primary lobbyists</a>&#8221; working to weaken the <span id=":23n.40" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Dodd</span>-Frank bill. Records <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=DABADD67-EFBB-4E12-9AA7-5461DE2139CB&amp;filingTypeID=69">show</a> that after the bill was signed into law, <span id=":23n.41" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Elmendorf</span> continued to work on behalf of a number of Wall Street clients to ensure the implementation was favorable to financial industry interests. <span id=":23n.42" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Elmendorf</span> was <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=1558228B-C27D-4F19-8694-B7CF4A41E634&amp;filingTypeID=51">tapped</a> by <span id=":23n.43" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Citigroup</span>, for example, to help the House of Representatives pass the Swap Jurisdiction Clarity Act, a bill strongly supported by Republican leadership in Congress to allow banks to avoid financial regulations by moving some operations overseas &#8212; a change that <a href="http://www.motherjones.com/mojo/2013/06/swap-jurisdiction-certainty-act-house-cross-border">experts say</a> could lead to another financial meltdown.</p>
<p>Elmendorf is one of many lobbyists who worked to influence Dodd-Frank now helping the Clinton campaign raise cash. Dewey co-founder Charles Baker worked on a lobbying team with <span id=":23n.65" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">DLA</span> Piper&#8217;s Matthew Bernstein, another major Clinton fundraiser, for Citizen Financial Group to help the bank <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=3cd35985-51d1-4f27-8bbc-8a24ebec23f4&amp;filingTypeID=51">lobby</a> on <span id=":23n.72" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Dodd</span>-Frank. Disclosures show the efforts included work on the Volcker Rule, derivatives regulations, and rules concerning overdraft fees, many of the top concerns for the banking industry. Arshi Siddiqui, a lobbyist with Akin Gump Strauss Hauer &amp; Feld who is currently helping to raise money for the Clinton campaign, worked to <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=5eee32d3-520c-4c50-85d8-48bf407c4928&amp;filingTypeID=80">influence</a> Dodd-Frank implementation on behalf of the Mortgage Bankers Association, according to disclosures. Tony <span id=":23n.86" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Podesta</span>, the brother of the Clinton campaign&#8217;s chairman and a <a href="http://docquery.fec.gov/cgi-bin/forms/C00575795/1047311/sa/3L">fundraiser</a> for the campaign, worked for Bank of America to influence <span id=":23n.89" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Dodd</span>-Frank, according to <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=3cb4970c-3141-4876-a945-067970cc9425&amp;filingTypeID=51"><span id=":23n.90" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">filings</span></a>.</p>
<p>Most of the Clinton campaign fundraisers who lobbied on Dodd-Frank did not respond to a request for comment. When asked about the work, Tony Podesta emailed us to say, &#8220;Call B of A.&#8221; An inquiry to Bank of America was not returned.</p>
<p>There are other lobbyists on the Clinton campaign staff. T. LaDavia Drane, the Clinton campaign&#8217;s director of African-American outreach, <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=7de05d96-ba11-480f-bfd6-1333efd43aa5&amp;filingTypeID=64">previously</a> worked as a lobbyist for a trade group that represents Pepsico and Hershey on issues related to obesity and advertising to children. Jeff Berman, a senior Clinton campaign official who is leading her <a href="http://www.buzzfeed.com/bensmith/hillary-clinton-hires-the-strategist-who-broke-her-2008-camp#.mdBvoAxZP">delegate strategy</a>, previously <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=190d2532-f2df-4efa-ac21-8911cb888bb3&amp;filingTypeID=53">worked</a> as a lobbyist for the private prison firm Geo Group, seeking to influence the federal budget, as well as working for TransCanada to help secure approval of the controversial Keystone XL pipeline.</p>
<p>Speaking to <em>The <span id=":23n.92" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Hill</span></em> last year, Tony <span id=":23n.93" class="J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc">Podesta</span> <a href="http://thehill.com/video/in-the-news/232867-dem-insider-obamas-lobbyist-ban-will-end-in-2017">said</a> that unlike President Obama, who instituted a ban on registered lobbyists in his administration, K Street will find a more welcome home in a Clinton White House.</p>
<p>&#8220;I think Hillary Clinton will be the next nominee and probably be the next president, but whomever the next president is will not maintain the lobbying ban,&#8221; he predicted. &#8220;It was a good applause line for Obama, but it didn&#8217;t seem to make much sense for policy.&#8221;</p>
<p><strong>Correction: February 9, 2016</strong></p>
<p><em>Due to an editing error, an earlier version of this story stated that the Financial Services Forum paid Benenson Strategy Group to lobby. The firm says it was paid to consult, not lobby.</em></p>

Medicare Advantage, which allows Medicare beneficiaries to use plans administered by private insurers, had long served as a cash cow for the health insurance industry. By one estimate, insurance companies over-billed the government by nearly \u003ca href=\"http://www.publicintegrity.org/2014/06/04/14840/why-medicare-advantage-costs-taxpayers-billions-more-it-should\">$70 billion\u003c/a> in improper payments over just a five year period. Dewey, which had been tapped to by health insurers to block cuts from the program starting in 2007, continued during the Obama era to lobby to protect Medicare Advantage, even as such reforms became a major part of how Democrats and the Obama administration sought to finance the Affordable Care Act.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>One of the more deceptive components of the Dewey lobbying strategy was uncovered when an editor at the Lawrence, Massachusetts, \u003cem>Eagle-Tribune \u003c/em>\u003ca href=\"http://www.cjr.org/campaign_desk/a_laurel_to_the_eagletribune.php\">realized\u003c/a> that the firm had worked quietly to place letters to the editor against cuts to Medicare Advantage under the names of elderly Massachusetts residents without their knowledge or consent.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>Last year, \u003cem>\u003cspan id=\":23n.8\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">ProPublica\u003c/span>\u003c/em>\u003cspan id=\":23n.8\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">’s\u003c/span> Alec \u003cspan id=\":23n.9\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">MacGillis\u003c/span> \u003ca href=\"https://www.propublica.org/article/insurance-lobby-has-sturdy-bridges-to-democrats\">pressed\u003c/a> America&#8217;s Health Insurance Plans, a major private health insurance lobby group that had retained Dewey, over the controversy. \u003cspan id=\":23n.10\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">AHIP&#8217;s\u003c/span> spokesperson brushed off the fake letter incident as the work of an intern and told \u003cspan id=\":23n.11\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">MacGillis\u003c/span> that \u003cspan id=\":23n.12\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">AHIP\u003c/span> ended its relationship with Dewey back in 2008, before the letters were sent. But tax documents reviewed by \u003cem>The Intercept\u003c/em> show that a state-level health insurance \u003ca href=\"http://www.mahp.com/about-us/mahp-board-members.html\">lobby group\u003c/a> called the Massachusetts Association of Health Plans, which represents the same major health insurance firms as \u003cspan id=\":23n.13\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">AHIP\u003c/span>, \u003ca href=\"http://pdfs.citizenaudit.org/2010_09_EO/22-2518016_990O_200912.pdf\">continued\u003c/a> to pay Dewey throughout 2009 to 2012 for &#8220;grassroots&#8221; consulting.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>&#8220;I don’t have any comment for your piece,&#8221; wrote Ginny Terzano, a spokesperson for Dewey, in response to an inquiry from \u003cem>The Intercept\u003c/em>.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>In 2009, Dewey was also retained by the National Restaurant Association, which at the time was lobbying aggressively to block health reform rules to require employers to provide health coverage to their employees. Charles Baker, the Dewey co-founder who now serves as a senior strategist to the Clinton campaign, was also \u003ca href=\"http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=ddb30cdf-ca82-4a82-96f9-6409b62bb7c1&amp;filingTypeID=51\">registered\u003c/a> to influence health reform on behalf of the Medicines Company, a drug firm.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>Other lobbyists now closely associated with the Clinton campaign were active in the fight against \u003cspan id=\":23n.14\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Obama&#8217;s\u003c/span> health reforms.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>Heather \u003cspan id=\":23n.15\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Podesta\u003c/span> is one of the most prolific fundraisers for the Clinton campaign, having personally raised at least \u003ca href=\"http://docquery.fec.gov/cgi-bin/forms/C00575795/1047311/sa/3L?platform=hootsuite\">$348,581\u003c/a>, according to recently filed disclosures. In 2009 and 2010, \u003cspan id=\":23n.16\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Podesta\u003c/span> worked as lobbyist for the health insurance company \u003cspan id=\":23n.17\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Cigna\u003c/span>. While working for \u003cspan id=\":23n.19\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Cigna to\u003c/span> \u003ca href=\"http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=A13D0957-6EA9-4038-8A3F-A62C51536DB8&amp;filingTypeID=69\">influence\u003c/a> the health reform bill, \u003cspan id=\":23n.20\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Cigna\u003c/span> was one of several large health insurers to secretly provide over \u003ca href=\"http://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2010-11-17/insurers-gave-u-s-chamber-86-million-used-to-oppose-obama-s-health-law\">$86 million\u003c/a> in secret payments used to air negative television and radio advertisements to defeat the law. Bueno and Podesta did not respond to a request for comment.\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>\u003cstrong>Helping big banks undermine financial reform\u003c/strong>\u003c/p>\n\u003cp>On financial reform, Clinton has similarly tied herself to \u003cspan id=\":23n.26\" class=\"J-JK9eJ-PJVNOc\">Obama&#8217;s\u003c/span> legacy.
