Editorial Reviews

Review

   To call this book a romance would be misleading, as it would be to call
   it a mystery; it is both and much more. And being tinged with a touch
   of 50 Shades of Grey certainly doesn't hurt. P.J. Fiala is to be
   commended for telling all of us over 50 that there is still an
   opportunity to find that soul mate and if you've already found him/her,
   that it is more than okay to experiment a little, try new things, take
   a few chances, and most of all trust your partner (especially in the
   bedroom). There's a lot of love in this book (between the two main
   characters, between friends, in-laws and the blended family), but
   there's also a lot of baggage that needs to be sorted out. Add to that
   someone who has (and still wants to) kill, and what more could you ask
   for. Reading Designing Samantha's Love is truly a thrilling ride from a
   stay-awake-all-night-to-see-what-happens kind of book.
   ~ Linda Thompson, host of TheAuthorsShow.com

From the Author

   I hope you love this book as much as I loved writing it.  I wanted to
   write a book about two people who have "been there, done that" and
   still hope to find love.  Thank you for your purchase.

More About the Author

   › [100]Visit Amazon's PJ Fiala Page
   PJ Fiala
   Top 5 Best Seller.
   I am a wife of thirty years, a mother of four grown children and the
   grandmother of three lovely grandchildren. When not writing a new
   story, I can be found riding my motorcycle and exploring this fabulous
   country of ours. My writing revolves around people anyone would love to
   spend time with. No self-absorbed billionaires for me.
   Earning my Bachelor's Degree later in life fulfilled a dream for me.
   Then, I found the courage to write and I haven't looked back. Currently
   I have three published books, Designing Samantha's Love, Dog Days of
   Summer and Rydin' the Storm Out. Danny's War will be coming soon. I
   also serve as the VP of Communications for WisRWA and devote a large
   amount of my time helping other authors slog their way through this
   thing called publishing. I love to hear from fans, so look me up and
   touch base.
