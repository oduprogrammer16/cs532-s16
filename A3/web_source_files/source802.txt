<p>Perform one to three circuits, resting three to five minutes between each. Do the workout once per week for six weeks, adding one rep to each exercise in every session. (So by Week 6, you should be able to do at least 10 reps per move.) DO IT! Perform these exercises back-to-back as quickly as possible. No rest! Complete at least five reps per move.</p>
<h2>Lock-off<img class="aligncenter wp-image-3414" src="http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Lock-off-pushup.jpg" alt="Lock-off-pushup" width="430" height="224" srcset="http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Lock-off-pushup-300x156.jpg 300w, http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Lock-off-pushup.jpg 558w" sizes="(max-width: 430px) 100vw, 430px" /></h2>
<p>Get into pushup position over a medicine ball or small box. Lower your body until your chest is just above the floor and then push back up. Reach up with your opposite hand and slap your chest. Do all your reps, then switch sides.</p>
<h2>Drop ’N’ pop</h2>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-3415" src="http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Drop-n-pop-pushup.jpg" alt="Drop-n-pop-pushup" width="325" height="335" srcset="http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Drop-n-pop-pushup-291x300.jpg 291w, http://fitmw.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/12/Drop-n-pop-pushup.jpg 363w" sizes="(max-width: 325px) 100vw, 325px" />From the top position of a close-grip pushup, drop so your chest touches the ball. Then explosively push yourself back up so your hands come up in the air. Catch yourself on the ball.</p>
