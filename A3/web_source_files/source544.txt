                    <p>The campaign of Republican presidential primaries candidate <strong>Donald Trump</strong> has put Memphis supporters on standby to expect a local rally sometime in the next few weeks.</p>
<p>Shelby County Commission chairman <strong>Terry Roland</strong>, who is the honorary chairman for Trump's West Tennessee operation, said he and other supporters were told at a meeting Friday that Trump would give them a 24-hour notice before he flies in for the rally.</p>
<p>Others at the meeting said they were told to expect the call in the next two weeks, but Roland said no definite time frame was given.</p>
<p>&quot;He's coming, but we actually don't know when he's coming,&quot; Roland said Monday.</p>
<p>Trump, a frontrunner among the Republicans who came in second place behind <strong>Ted Cruz</strong> in the Iowa caucuses last week, has drawn large crowds elsewhere, including at a rally in Little Rock last Wednesday. Roland said he expects the rally to draw a large crowd, and that he wasn't sure yet where the rally would be held.</p>
