<h2><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">The father of a 12-year-old Pennsylvania girl who was killed while a constable was serving an eviction notice has been charged with criminal homicide in his daughter&#8217;s death, prosecutors said.</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">Don Meyer allegedly pointed a loaded semi-automatic assault rifle at the chest of Constable Clarke Steele as they argued over an order to vacate on January 11. More after the cut&#8230;<span id="more-12980"></span></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">Steele fired one round, which struck the 57-year-old father in the arm and then pierced the chest of his daughter, Ciara, who was hiding behind him. She died at the scene. She had been home from school that day because she was sick. Prosecutors upgraded charges against Meyer on Thursday. He now faces counts including criminal homicide, involuntary manslaughter, aggravated assault, endangering the welfare of children and illegal possession of weapons.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">He is being held without bail in Pennsylvania&#8217;s Perry County Jail and has pleaded not guilty. Several eviction orders had been issued against Meyer, authorities said, and he had been notified by telephone that a constable was on the way to serve him with another notice. Meyer met the constable at the door of his apartment with the rifle slung over his shoulder, authorities said. Meyer allegedly raised the loaded weapon and pointed it at Steele&#8217;s chest as the two argued.</span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"><em>“The constable was there the day before to remind them that the next day was the day they had to be out,” family member Hope Cordas Rohde said after the shooting. “Don had an arsenal of weapons and he had them ready. He wasn’t going to pay,” she said.</em></span></p></blockquote>
