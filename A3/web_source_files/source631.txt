			<h1><a rel="bookmark" href="http://ageofaquarius.tumblr.com/post/138935119483/masks-miscues-mayhem">Masks, Miscues, &amp; Mayhem</a></h1>
		</header>
		<section>
			<p><a href="http://smoakandarrow.tumblr.com/post/138933778814/masks-miscues-mayhem" class="tumblr_blog">smoakandarrow</a>:</p>

<blockquote><p>Whew.&nbsp; Did I start a kerfuffle the other day or what?&nbsp; As I mentioned on Twitter, that format isn&rsquo;t the best to clear up miscommunications or misunderstandings.&nbsp; Blogs are.&nbsp; So here we go.</p><p> It all started with DC&rsquo;s tweet about their All Access interview with Emily Bett <br></p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="614" data-orig-width="603"><img data-orig-height="614" data-orig-width="603" alt="image" src="http://40.media.tumblr.com/6bb53ba729e9e4e305aa4bdb8ba9ce4f/tumblr_inline_o27llzd91w1rkf4dx_500.png" width="500" height="509"></figure><p>and my response:</p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="96" data-orig-width="614"><img data-orig-height="96" data-orig-width="614" alt="image" src="http://41.media.tumblr.com/cf6ce869b73a38ae3de309a64f8c07d3/tumblr_inline_o27lme7yTK1rkf4dx_500.png" width="500" height="78"></figure><p>To save people the chore of tagging <a href="http://tmblr.co/mdJEFbKW63vxPcpiMT-fLuA">@marcguggenheim</a> on this post or selectively screencapping it for him.&nbsp; There.&nbsp; Tagged him.&nbsp; Figured it was just easier this way, Marc.&nbsp; I&rsquo;m nothing if not transparent.&nbsp; Whatever I say on any of my social media accounts I would gladly say face to face for discussion.&nbsp; I&rsquo;m all about discussion.&nbsp; Good?&nbsp; Good.&nbsp; <br></p><p>For the purposes of this post, &ldquo;mask&rdquo; refers to a hero suit, mask, or combo of both.&nbsp; It&rsquo;s just a lazy shortcut for me cause I don&rsquo;t want to keep having to type mask and/or suit.</p><p>Oh, one more thing: I have never, <i>ever</i>, said I speak for anyone, let alone the fandom. Ever.&nbsp; I speak for myself.&nbsp; Period.&nbsp; My opinions and the things I say are <i><b>mine</b></i>.&nbsp; This is why I encourage everyone to be vocal for themselves.&nbsp; Is everyone going to always agree on things?&nbsp; Of course not.&nbsp; That&rsquo;s why it&rsquo;s called opinion.&nbsp; <br></p><hr><p>Now, before we get into what I <i>was</i> driving at in my tweet, let&rsquo;s be super clear about what I was <i><b>not </b></i>saying.&nbsp; <br></p><h2><b>I was absolutely<i> NOT </i>saying:</b></h2><p>1. Felicity Smoak is not a hero if she doesn&rsquo;t have a mask! <br></p><p>2. Felicity Smoak won&rsquo;t be recognized by others as a hero until she has a mask&hellip; so give her a mask!<br></p><p>3. I want/need/crave Felicity Smoak as a masked hero. It must happen!&nbsp; It <i>must</i>!!! {hurls self on ground sobbing over her lack of maskdom} <br></p><p>4. I want Felicity Smoak to get a mask just so she can be in posters, ads, and other promotion<br></p><hr><p>I think that&rsquo;s clear enough.&nbsp; Everybody gets what I was <b><i>not</i> </b>saying, right?&nbsp; Cool.&nbsp; So set all those out of our brains for the rest of this discussion.&nbsp; This is a conversation &ndash; with questions &ndash; about a topic that&rsquo;s popped up repeatedly through the seasons.&nbsp; All I&rsquo;m doing is phrasing &amp; presenting it a <i>different </i>way and I think that&rsquo;s where the short-circuiting began.</p><p>Some of these questions were asked when CW released this promo:</p><figure class="tmblr-embed tmblr-full"><iframe id="youtube_iframe" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/p1P7ujKw9yQ?feature=oembed&amp;enablejsapi=1&amp;origin=https://safe.txmblr.com&amp;wmode=opaque" frameborder="0" height="281" width="500"></iframe></figure><p>They were asked when CW had this Heroes/Villains panel and <a href="https://t.co/YHAjX26lid">looked like this</a>.&nbsp; And yes, David Ramsey was there, but he also teased discussion of Diggle getting some sort of suit.<br></p><p>They were asked <i>again </i>when this printed promotional material came out and popped up at cons and other promotional opportunities.</p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="438" data-orig-width="598"><img data-orig-height="438" data-orig-width="598" alt="image" src="http://40.media.tumblr.com/a661bfe55cf61d531ddfd72e23e95768/tumblr_inline_o27ttwOCbY1rkf4dx_500.png" width="500" height="366"></figure><p>That question was: Why, if the CW Network and the Arrow show runners say, &ldquo;You don&rsquo;t need a mask to be a hero,&rdquo; are the <i>only </i>people marketed/promoted as heroes by CW the ones in masks?</p><p>Keep this question in mind.&nbsp; Also keep in mind that Arrow show runners don&rsquo;t control network promotion.<br></p><p>The other day, Marc graciously answered an Ask I sent him.</p><figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-height="502" data-orig-width="886"><img data-orig-height="502" data-orig-width="886" alt="image" src="http://41.media.tumblr.com/b0d1a9039e15aa855c997f8d0ffaf96b/tumblr_inline_o27npmmddg1rkf4dx_500.png" width="500" height="283"></figure><p>I think I&rsquo;m pretty clear in my ask, right?&nbsp; I&rsquo;m following up a point DC Comics itself raised in their interview with Emily.&nbsp; I&rsquo;m <i>philosophically</i> musing the issue of &ldquo;Why not Felicity Smoak?&rdquo; and I wanted to know his thoughts on Pros &amp; Cons of suiting her up.&nbsp; I think Marc is very clear in agreeing 100% that a hero does not need a mask and that Felicity Smoak IS a hero.&nbsp; I think we all agree, right?&nbsp; Right.</p><p>Am I outraged over no suits/mask for Felicity?&nbsp; Pfft. Absolutely not. {points to #1-4 above}.&nbsp; I don&rsquo;t even <i>care </i>if she gets a mask for crying out loud. <b>The mask is <i>not&nbsp; </i>the point.&nbsp; </b>This is not a rant.&nbsp; I am not angry.<b>&nbsp;</b> What I am is trying to open a dialogue and discussion about Felicity Smoak<b> </b>because I&rsquo;m <i>curious</i>.<b>&nbsp; </b>When I want to know something: I ask.<b><br></b></p><p>Now, bring all this together with my response to DC Comics&rsquo; tweet and when apparently everything hit the fan in a jumble of Wha?&nbsp; <br></p><h2><b>What HAVE I been Saying / Asking</b><br></h2><p>My question is simple.&nbsp; Why Not Felicity?&nbsp; Why?<i>&nbsp; </i>Why,<i> from a show structure stance, </i><b>not </b>Felicity Smoak?<i>&nbsp; </i><b>This &ldquo;Why Not&ldquo; question is not about the mask.</b>&nbsp; It&rsquo;s about the entire landscape and philosophy behind the choices with this particular character by the show &amp; network vs. other characters.</p><p>I ask <i>Why Not Felicity?</i> not because she &ldquo;has to have&rdquo; a mask but because - in a comic book based show - where everyone (okay, exception of Quentin noted)&hellip; <i>everyone </i>has become a masked hero&hellip; <i>Why not Felicity?</i>&nbsp; Why is <i>she </i>where the &ldquo;Never!&rdquo; line is apparently drawn? Why is the answer, for her, a &ldquo;it will probably be after I leave the show&rdquo; when practically every other question, idea,&nbsp; and proposition for other characters is almost always answered with, &ldquo;Never say Never&rdquo; and &ldquo;it will happen if we can find an organic way to go there&rdquo;?&nbsp; Hell, even the No Goatee stance has recently been abandoned.<br></p><p>So&hellip;<i> Why not Felicity?<br></i></p><p>Look, I love Felicity Smoak.&nbsp; I <i>adore </i>her.&nbsp; I love her for who she is on every level.&nbsp; I also love her stunts.&nbsp; I love her undercover missions.&nbsp; I love when she gets to go into the field to help blow up buildings, take down a master jewel thief, thwart Slade Wilson, and jump out of airplanes.&nbsp; Remember all that?&nbsp; Of course you do.&nbsp; It was fun stuff!&nbsp; <br></p><p>Season 3 didn&rsquo;t give her <i>any </i>of that.&nbsp; Dark days.&nbsp; Boo.&nbsp; <br></p><p>Now, in Season 4, she&rsquo;s paralyzed and obviously won&rsquo;t be doing any of that stuff for awhile, if ever (cue dramatic music).&nbsp; Could this still be great story for her?&nbsp; Of course!&nbsp; It&rsquo;s why I&rsquo;m waiting to see how it all plays out.&nbsp; There&rsquo;s great setup with her dad and so many things.&nbsp; I&rsquo;m a story whore.&nbsp; Bring it.</p><p>BUT.&nbsp; This does <i>not </i>prevent me from asking questions <i>now</i> because, while the current situation obviously has great story implications, it also has deeper, ongoing issues that &ndash; while unpleasant &ndash; still have to be considered.</p><p>Felicity designed tech.&nbsp; But not anymore.&nbsp; Cause people with masks/mask destinies get to do that now.</p><p>Felicity reconstructed the lair multiple times.&nbsp; But not anymore.&nbsp; Cause people with masks/mask destinies get to do that now. Yes, she &ldquo;gave input&rdquo; but still it was made clear: Mask.</p><p>Felicity is tasked with saving her company.&nbsp; But doesn&rsquo;t.&nbsp; Cause somebody with a mask destiny (no we haven&rsquo;t seen said mask, but yes, they&rsquo;ve made it clear it&rsquo;s coming) got to do it.&nbsp; But hey, Felicity got to give a Powerpoint presentation about the brilliance of masked &amp; soon-to-be-masked people&rsquo;s achievements and contributions.</p><p>Felicity used to do stunts.&nbsp; But doesn&rsquo;t anymore.&nbsp; Cause it&rsquo;s not organic.&nbsp; But&hellip; masks. <br></p><p>Felicity used to go into the field.&nbsp; But doesn&rsquo;t anymore.&nbsp; Cause it&rsquo;s not organic.&nbsp; But&hellip; masks. <br></p><p>Felicity &ldquo;used to&rdquo; do <i>a lot</i> of things that are now &ldquo;masked&rdquo; assigned, organic for them, but not for her.&nbsp; So no, <b>Felicity Smoak does not need a mask</b>, but the question must be asked: Does not having a mask mean she&rsquo;s losing out on things bestowed upon those who <i>are</i>?<br></p><p>Wait! I hear you in the back!&nbsp; <br></p><p>&ldquo;Felicity Smoak doesn&rsquo;t need a mask!&nbsp; She&rsquo;s a hacker &amp; doesn&rsquo;t even go on missions.&nbsp; She stays in the lair and nobody sees her!&rdquo;<br></p><p>Yes.&nbsp; Exactly.&nbsp; <i>Absorb</i> <i>that for a moment</i>: Felicity Smoak <i>never </i>goes on missions anymore.</p><p>Since when?</p><figure class="tmblr-full"  as much (if not <i>more</i>) than some characters. &nbsp;She has to be <i>allowed
 </i>to grow. &nbsp;She cannot remain static &ldquo;because it&rsquo;s Felicity.&rdquo;<br></p><p><i>Is</i> she even being handled differently?&nbsp; Is it &ldquo;perception&rdquo;?&nbsp; Maybe.&nbsp; <br></p><p>Does <i>any</i> of this mean the current story for Felicity Smoak won&rsquo;t be awesome?&nbsp; Of course not!&nbsp; <br></p><p>Does it mean that any of these choices in this story (paralysis, wheelchair, etc) were made for nefarious reasons?&nbsp; Of course not!&nbsp; <br></p><p>Does it mean I&rsquo;m not excited for all the possibilities, her father, and what&rsquo;s to come for Felicity Smoak or feel that Felicity Smoak isn&rsquo;t freaking awesome?&nbsp; Hell no.&nbsp; I&rsquo;m in my chair watching every week!&nbsp; Let&rsquo;s see where this roller coaster ride goes!<br></p><p>However, this also <i>does not</i> mean that people with these questions &ndash; people like me &ndash; shouldn&rsquo;t<i> </i>be <i>asking </i>Arrow TPTB about this stuff *<b>NOW,</b>* especially since the EPs have already said they&rsquo;re talking Season 5 plans.</p><p>If nobody asked these questions &ndash; if <i>nobody </i>ever says,&nbsp;&ldquo;Hey, do you realize that (true or not) the <i>perception</i> for some of this stuff comes across as&hellip;&rdquo; &ndash; until <i>after </i>Season 4 is over, it&rsquo;s already way <b><i>too late.</i></b><br></p><p>&ldquo;But Felicity doesn&rsquo;t <i>need </i>to change!&rdquo; people tell me.&nbsp; &ldquo;She&rsquo;s perfect the way she is!&rdquo;&nbsp; Of course she doesn&rsquo;t &ldquo;need to change.&rdquo; She <i>is</i> amazing.&nbsp; But she <i>does </i>need to evolve as a character.&nbsp; Every character does and has on Arrow.&nbsp; Every single one of them.&nbsp; They&rsquo;ve been given that opportunity, whether you feel they earned that right or not.</p><p>Felicity has made some changes, but there have been setbacks as well, perceived stripping of skill-sets that feel granted to other (masked/destined to be masked) people.&nbsp; <br></p><p>Felicity Smoak cannot be the exception to the rules the network/show applies to<i> everybody </i>else.&nbsp; That is all I have been trying to say.&nbsp; And if Felicity doesn&rsquo;t get those opportunities, if she is denied everything else I&rsquo;ve discussed above?&nbsp; Say it with me:</p><h2>Why <i>Not</i> Felicity Smoak?</h2></blockquote>
