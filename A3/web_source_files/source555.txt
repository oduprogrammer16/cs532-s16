<h1 class="omc-post-heading-standard">3 Secrets to Funding Success in Startup Tech Companies</h1>

    <p class="no-break"><span class='st_sharethis_hcount' st_title='3 Secrets to Funding Success in Startup Tech Companies' st_url='http://www.buildbrandblast.com/3-secrets-to-funding-success-in-startup-tech-companies/'></span><br />

<h3><i>Getting funding is one of the biggest hurdles you will ever overcome as a business, and these three little secrets can help ensure you get what you need.</i></h3>
<p>For some entrepreneurs, getting funding is a nightmare. It’s certainly stressful and requires a heck of a lot of preparation. <b>Your entire idea is often on the line</b>, based on whether your target investors think your idea is worth investing in, and if you mess up your presentation you might have to start from scratch looking for funding elsewhere.</p>
<p>There are a lot of different ways to pursue funding for your startup tech company, including looking for angel investors, venture capitalists, or a crowdfunding campaign, but all of them offer a similar platform for your pitch. As a serial entrepreneur, I’ve faced my share of challenges getting and keeping funding, and as a result, I’ve been able to identify <b>three key secrets</b> in getting the funding you need, no matter what the source is:</p>
<ol>
<li><b>Have a concise pitch. </b>You need to know your idea inside and out, even if you have plans for the next 5-10 years. But you don’t want to tell your investors everything at once. The best ideas for startups are those that can be effectively described in just one or two sentences—so make sure you can be at least that concise.</li>
<li><b>Care. </b>You won’t get anything from anybody unless you genuinely care and are visibly passionate about your ideas. Don’t go overboard with expressing your affections, but don’t present cold either. Make sure your investors know how passionate and committed you are to your ideas.</li>
<li><b>Have your research done. </b>You’re probably going to face a lot of questions from your potential investors, so make sure you know your audience, your market, your competitors, and your overall strategy. The more well-read you can appear, the better.</li>
</ol>
<p>That’s it. Really. If you can be ready with those three secrets to a masterful funding pitch, you’ll have just about everything you need to get investment capital. Depending on the investor you’re pursuing, you’ll have to make some adjustments, and of course you’ll have plenty of practicing to do, but the fundamentals always come first.</p>
