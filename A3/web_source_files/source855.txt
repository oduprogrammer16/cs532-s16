			                <section class="article-content" itemprop="articleBody">
                    <figure class="aligncenter"><img title="Big Hero 6" src=" http://media.comicbook.com/2016/02/big-hero-6-168820.jpg" alt="Big Hero 6" width="655" height="380" data-item=" 168820" /><figcaption><span class="meta">(Photo: Disney Animation)</span></figcaption></figure>
<p>Daniel Gerson, the co-screenwriter of Disney and Marvel&rsquo;s<a href="http://comicbook.com/category/big-hero-6"> <em>Big Hero 6</em></a> and a contributor to several Pixar films, has died at age 49.</p>
<p>Gerson died of brain cancer in Los Angeles on Saturday,&nbsp;<a href="http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/nytimes/obituary.aspx?n=DANIEL-GERSON&pid=177635394">his family announced.</a></p>
<iframe class="cb_embed" src="http://comicbook.com/category/big-hero-6/embed" width="600" height="282"></iframe>
<p>Gerson and his writing partner, Robet L. Baird, co-wrote Pixar&rsquo;s <em>Monsters Inc.</em> and the 2013 prequel <em>Monsters University</em>. The writing duo is also credited for 2017&rsquo;s <em>Cars 3</em>. Other credits include <em>Chicken Little</em>, <em>Cars</em>, <em>Open Season</em>, <em>Meet the Robinsons</em>, <em>Up</em>, and <em>Inside Out</em>.</p>
<p>Baird also provided the voice of a few small roles in his own films, including janitors Needleman and Smitty in <em>Monsters Inc.</em> and the desk sergeant in <em>Big Hero 6</em>.</p>
<p>Gerson is survived by his wife of 20 years and two children.</p>
<p>Via&nbsp;<a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/daniel-gerson-dead-monsters-screenwriter-862674?utm_source=twitter">THR</a></p>
