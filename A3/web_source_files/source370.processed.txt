Watch: ‘Jason Bourne’ First Trailer Drops During Super Bowl

   February 7, 2016 | 04:28PM PT

[45]Alex Stedman

   News Editor, Variety.com [46]@a_sted

   The first trailer for [47]Matt Damon’s “[48]Jason Bourne” debuted
   during [49]Super Bowl 50, giving fans a look at the upcoming thriller.

   The sequel sees Damon and director Paul Greengrass (who helmed “The
   Bourne Supremacy” and “The Bourne Ultimatum”) re-teaming to bring the
   famous spy out of retirement. It’s been nine years since Bourne
   appeared on the big screen — the upcoming installment marks the fifth
   film in the franchise.

   “I know who I am,” says Damon in the clip. “I remember everything.”

   The series started in 2002, with “The Bourne Identity.” The last movie
   in the series came in 2012 with “The Bourne Legacy.” However, neither
   Bourne nor Damon appeared in that film. Jeremy Renner starred as Black
   ops agent Aaron Cross.

   See More:[50]Watch: ‘X-Men: Apocalypse’ Trailer Sees Mutants Go to War

   Julia Stiles is set to return to the series, reprising her role as
   Nicky Parsons. “Ex Machina” and “The Danish Girl” actress Alicia
   Vikander, Tommy Lee Jones and Vincent Cassel will make their debuts in
   the franchise.

   Frank Marshall is producing “Jason Bourne” alongside Jeffrey Weiner for
   Captivate Entertainment. Greengrass, Damon, Greg Goodman and Ben Smith
   also produce, with Christopher Rouse and Henry Morrison serving as exec
   [51]producers.

   The trailer also marks the reveal of the movie’s official title, which
   had been kept secret ahead of the Big Game. “Jason Bourne” will hit
   theaters on July 29.
