Twitter says it’s ‘suspended over 125,000 accounts’ related to terrorism

   [90]Jordan Novet February 5, 2016 12:22 PM
   Tags: [91]Isis, [92]terrorism, [93]Twitter
   Twitter's logo featured in the lobby of its San Francisco headquarters.

   Above: Twitter's logo featured in the lobby of its San Francisco
   headquarters.
   Image Credit: Ken Yeung/VentureBeat

   Twitter today said it has suspended more than 125,000 accounts that
   were used to make threats about or promote terrorist activity,
   especially as it pertains to ISIS.

   “Like most people around the world, we are horrified by the atrocities
   perpetrated by extremist groups. We condemn the use of Twitter to
   promote terrorism and the Twitter Rules make it clear that this type of
   behavior, or any violent threat, is not permitted on our service,”
   Twitter said in a [94]blog post. “As the nature of the terrorist threat
   has changed, so has our ongoing work in this area.”

   The effort comes after Telegram [95]announced that it had blocked 78
   channels that had engaged in ISIS-related activity.

   People affiliated with ISIS have previously [96]shared pictures and
   other content, hence promoting the group’s cause to millions.

   Twitter said today it had increased the size of teams who look into
   reports about Twitter activity related to terrorism and thereby has
   begun to respond to these reports faster.

   The company noted that “there is no ‘magic algorithm’ for identifying
   terrorist content on the Internet.” That’s a fascinating admission
   given Twitter’s collection of people skilled in machine learning. So
   human curation is the area in which Twitter is doubling down in order
   to deal with terrorist rhetoric on the social network.

   Last month Twitter [97]updated its rules around hateful conduct. “You
   may not make threats of violence or promote violence, including
   threatening or promoting terrorism,” one rule states.
