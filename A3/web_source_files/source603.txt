		<p>ST. LOUIS (KTVI) – The St. Louis Circuit Attorney’s Office charged a 17-year-old Friday in connection with the shooting death of another teenager late last month.</p>
<p>According to Schron Jackson, a spokeswoman for the St. Louis Metropolitan Police Department, said the murder took place just after 1 p.m. on January 30 outside the Bank of America in the 9000 block of Goodfellow Boulevard.</p>
<p>Officers found the victim, identified as 17-year-old Trevon Chapple, on the sidewalk with a gunshot wound to the head. Chapple was pronounced dead at the scene.</p>
<p>Investigators determined Chapple was shot inside a car, but managed to get out of the vehicle before succumbing to his injuries.</p>
<p>The suspect, Montrell Jeffrey, was charged with first-degree murder, first-degree robbery, and two counts of armed criminal action. Joffrey remains jailed on $1 million cash-only bond.</p>
